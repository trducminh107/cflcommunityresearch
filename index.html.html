Insights & Recommended Actions
Market-by-market assessment with prioritised action items based on community data analysis
🇮🇩 Indonesia ● Healthy
Star market — sustain and scale what's working
9/10
Health Score
Indonesia is the strongest market with 2,531 total engagement — 63% of all engagement across 3 markets combined. A 96% fill rate confirms the community is extremely active. User behaviour revolves around 3 clear pillars: Reward/Diamond, Tournament, and Game Meta — a highly effective retention triangle. Moderators are doing an excellent job organising events and driving two-way discussion.

Note: the keyword "March" appearing very frequently suggests heavy event activity in March — momentum needs to be sustained into April and beyond. Bug/complaint posts (41 total) should be monitored before they escalate.
Recommended Actions
01
Maintain bi-weekly event cadence. Valentine and Lunar New Year events drove the highest engagement. Apply cultural calendar marketing — Eid/Lebaran, Independence Day (Aug 17) — for upcoming events to keep the community activated.
Ongoing
02
Scale the Top Contributor program. Winner announcement posts reached 68 comments. Add gamification layers: weekly leaderboard, community badges, exclusive diamond rewards for the top 10 contributors each month.
Medium
03
Create an official structured feedback channel. 41 complaint posts about bugs/meta are scattered across the feed. Run a monthly pinned "Community Aspirations" post to consolidate feedback in one place, rather than letting it spread as noise.
Medium
04
Develop a multi-tier esports league system. Tournament/Competition is the #1 activity category. Build a tiered league structure (Bronze → Silver → Garudaku Pro) to give competitive players a clear progression path and reason to stay active.
Strategic
🇵🇭 Philippines ● Critical
Urgent attention needed — high potential, very low activation
4/10
Health Score
Philippines has a serious content volume problem: 58.5% of posts contain no text — the highest blank-post rate across all 3 markets. This signals the group is being used primarily to share images or videos without captions, or that members are not encouraged to write.

A key paradox: the single highest-performing post across all 3 markets belongs to Philippines — 177 engagements on a milestone reward post. This reveals enormous untapped potential. Philippines users respond powerfully to rewards and public recognition, but these triggers are not being used consistently by admins.

Characteristic behaviours: creator-oriented (TikTok/Reels hashtag usage), community verification-seeking ("ptpa" = please verify for me), and heavy clan recruitment — all pointing to social bonding as the primary engagement driver.
Recommended Actions
01
Launch recurring Milestone Campaigns immediately. The milestone reward post achieved 177 engagements — the highest across all markets. Schedule milestone posts every 2 weeks with a UID claim mechanism. This is the fastest lever to boost engagement right now.
Urgent
02
Launch an official Creator Program. Philippines has an active creator base using #cflcreator and #CFLTacticalUnit. Introduce a verified creator badge and exclusive perks (early content access, diamond bonuses) to keep local influencers loyal to the group.
High
03
Enforce a content caption policy. 117 of 200 posts have no text. Set a clear rule requiring at least one sentence of caption per post. Moderators should pin the guideline and actively react to quality posts to reinforce the expected behaviour.
High
04
Increase MVP recognition from Monthly to Weekly. The March MVP post performed well (27 engagements). Increasing recognition frequency will maintain momentum — Philippines users are particularly responsive to public recognition and social validation.
Medium
🌐 Global ● Moderate
Needs repositioning — community purpose is currently undefined
5/10
Health Score
The Global group has an identity crisis: the dominant content type (after uncategorised) is account buying/selling — not the intended purpose of a community group. Multiple Philippines-language posts are appearing here, suggesting weak content moderation.

The highest-performing post — "DEDGEYMMMM" (79 engagements) — is a meme with no substantive content. Both admin-application posts (61 engagements each) went viral due to humour, not information value. This shows the community is seeking entertainment more than information.

A growing "dead game" narrative in comments (comments outnumber likes 1.7:1) could damage player retention if left unaddressed.
Recommended Actions
01
Define and pin a clear group purpose. The group currently has no identity. Decide: Is it a global news hub? An English-language community? A cross-market hub? Pin an announcement with group rules and focus areas, then enforce them consistently.
Urgent
02
Ban account trading posts and enforce it. Account buy/sell is the most common non-admin post type. This harms the game ecosystem by encouraging account sharing/selling. Set a strict auto-removal rule and pin a clear warning.
High
03
Counter negative sentiment with positive content. Create a weekly "Good News" post: patch highlights, upcoming events, player success stories. Proactively flooding the feed with positive signal will dilute the growing "dead game" narrative.
Medium
04
Leverage the meme culture intentionally. "DEDGEYMMMM" went viral because of entertainment value. Create an official weekly fun/meme slot (e.g. "Friday Fun") to channel that energy into branded content rather than random uncontrolled posts.
Low
